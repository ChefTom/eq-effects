# Method Selection

## Overview

Method Selection determines the numerical or analytical approach used to solve the Mathematical Object.

---

## Purpose

To ensure that the chosen method:

- aligns with the structure of the problem
- satisfies precision requirements
- maintains numerical stability

---

## Method Types

Examples include:

- direct analytical methods
- numerical approximation methods
- iterative solvers
- domain-specific techniques

---

## Selection Criteria

The system considers:

- equation structure
- boundary conditions
- domain context
- precision requirements
- computational complexity

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- analyzes the Equation Graph
- evaluates candidate methods
- selects the most appropriate approach
- configures the method for execution

---

## Characteristics

- adaptive
- context-aware
- deterministic within defined conditions

---

## Role in Execution

Method Selection defines how the Mathematical Object is transformed into computable operations.

---

## Outcome

Method Selection ensures that the problem is solved using a **valid and appropriate computational approach**.
