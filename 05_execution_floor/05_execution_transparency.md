# Execution Transparency

## Overview

Execution Transparency ensures that all aspects of computation are visible and understandable.

---

## Purpose

To provide:

- insight into how results were produced
- confidence in computational correctness
- support for validation and reproducibility

---

## Transparency Components

The system exposes:

- selected methods (high-level)
- execution pathways
- applied constraints and assumptions
- precision settings
- validation outcomes

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- records execution details
- structures information for interpretation
- includes transparency data in the Rich Result Package

---

## Characteristics

- structured
- inspectable
- consistent

---

## Limitations

Transparency is provided at a level that:

- supports understanding
- avoids unnecessary implementation detail

---

## Importance

Without transparency:

- results cannot be validated effectively
- trust in computation is reduced
- debugging and refinement are difficult

---

## Outcome

Execution Transparency ensures that:

- computation is understandable
- results are trustworthy
- users can refine models with confidence
