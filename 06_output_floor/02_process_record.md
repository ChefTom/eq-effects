# Process Record

## Overview

The Process Record captures the sequence of steps taken during execution.

It provides a structured account of how the result was produced.

---

## Purpose

To enable:

- traceability
- validation
- reproducibility
- debugging and refinement

---

## Contents

The Process Record includes:

- selected methods (high-level)
- execution stages
- transformations applied to the Mathematical Object
- key decision points

---

## Characteristics

- chronological
- structured
- inspectable

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- records execution steps as they occur
- organizes them into a coherent sequence
- includes them in the Rich Result Package

---

## Role in the System

The Process Record connects:

- input (intent and structure)
- execution (methods and computation)
- output (results)

---

## Importance

Without a Process Record:

- results cannot be fully understood
- errors are difficult to diagnose
- reproducibility is limited

---

## Outcome

The Process Record provides a **clear and structured view of computation**, enabling deeper insight into how results are generated.
