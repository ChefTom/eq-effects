# Assumptions, Constraints, and Units

## Overview

This component defines the conditions under which the mathematical problem is valid.

It ensures that all elements of the system operate within a consistent framework.

---

## Assumptions

Assumptions define:

- simplifications
- approximations
- modeling choices

Examples include:
- steady-state vs transient behavior
- linear vs non-linear models
- idealized conditions

---

## Constraints

Constraints define:

- limits on variables
- boundary conditions
- invariants that must be satisfied

These may be:
- explicitly provided
- inferred from context

---

## Units and Dimensions

All variables are associated with:

- units of measurement
- dimensional relationships

The system ensures:
- dimensional consistency
- compatibility between equations

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- aggregates assumptions and constraints
- validates consistency across inputs
- identifies conflicts or missing definitions
- enforces dimensional correctness

---

## Importance

Without explicit assumptions and constraints:

- results may be invalid
- interpretation becomes unreliable
- reproducibility is compromised

---

## Outcome

This component produces a **consistent and validated set of conditions** that define the problem space.
