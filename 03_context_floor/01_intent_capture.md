# Intent Capture

## Overview

Intent Capture defines how EQ Effects identifies and formalizes the user’s objective.

It is the first step in transforming input into a structured representation.

---

## Purpose

To extract:

- what is being modeled
- what outcomes are expected
- what conditions must be satisfied

---

## Input Sources

Intent is derived from:

- conceptual conversation
- structured mathematical input
- contextual declarations

---

## Components of Intent

Intent is represented through:

- **Objective** — the goal of the computation
- **Scope** — the domain and boundaries of the problem
- **Conditions** — requirements and constraints
- **Expectations** — accuracy, behavior, or outcomes

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- parses input across all paths
- identifies key objectives
- constructs a structured intent model
- requests clarification when intent is incomplete or inconsistent

---

## Challenges

Intent may be:

- incomplete
- ambiguous
- expressed at varying levels of detail

The system must reconcile these differences.

---

## Outcome

Intent Capture produces a **formalized representation of user goals** that guides all subsequent processing.
