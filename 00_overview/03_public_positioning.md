# Public Positioning

## Overview

EQ Effects is positioned as a **system-level architecture** for mathematical computation.

It is designed to integrate with existing compute ecosystems while introducing a new model for how mathematical work is defined and executed.

---

## Positioning Statement

EQ Effects is a governed mathematical execution environment where:

- users define intent and form
- the system structures that intent
- the computer performs the solve
- results are returned with full transparency

---

## Relationship to Existing Systems

EQ Effects works alongside:

- CPU and GPU compute environments
- numerical libraries and solvers
- high-performance computing systems
- emerging compute architectures

It does not replace these systems.

It governs how they are used.

---

## Target Domains

EQ Effects is applicable to:

- scientific research
- engineering and simulation
- financial modeling
- optimization and systems analysis
- emerging computational fields

---

## Key Differentiator

EQ Effects allows users to operate at the level of:

> conceptual reasoning and mathematical form

without requiring translation into code.

---

## Value Proposition

- Reduced abstraction loss between idea and execution
- Increased transparency in computational processes
- Improved reproducibility and auditability
- Flexibility across domains and compute platforms

---

## Audience

This repository is intended for:

- scientists and mathematicians
- engineers and system designers
- academic institutions
- compute and hardware partners
- funding and research organizations

---

## Outcome

EQ Effects introduces a new model for computation:

> from tool-driven workflows  
to  
> intent-driven, governed execution
