# Precision Governance

## Overview

Precision Governance defines how numerical accuracy is managed throughout computation.

It ensures that results meet required levels of fidelity while maintaining computational efficiency.

---

## Purpose

To control:

- numerical precision
- stability of computation
- allocation of computational resources

---

## Precision Model

Precision may be applied:

- globally across the entire problem
- locally within specific regions
- adaptively based on problem characteristics

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- interprets user-defined precision requirements
- applies domain-appropriate precision strategies
- adjusts precision dynamically where necessary

---

## Localized Precision

Certain regions of a problem may require:

- higher numerical fidelity
- increased computational resolution

The system allocates precision accordingly.

---

## Characteristics

- adaptive
- context-aware
- resource-efficient

---

## Role in the System

Precision Governance influences:

- method selection
- solver configuration
- compute mapping

---

## Importance

Improper precision can lead to:

- inaccurate results
- instability
- loss of trust in computation

---

## Outcome

Precision Governance ensures that computation is performed at the **appropriate level of accuracy**, aligned with both user intent and system requirements.
