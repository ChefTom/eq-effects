# Failure Modes

## Overview

Failure Modes define the conditions under which EQ Effects cannot proceed or cannot guarantee valid results.

---

## Purpose

To ensure that:

- invalid computations are prevented
- users are informed of limitations
- system behavior remains predictable

---

## Types of Failure

### Incomplete Input
- missing variables
- undefined relationships
- insufficient constraints

---

### Conflicting Definitions
- contradictory constraints
- incompatible assumptions
- inconsistent units

---

### Unresolved Ambiguity
- multiple valid interpretations
- insufficient clarification

---

### Numerical Instability
- ill-conditioned systems
- divergence in iterative methods
- sensitivity beyond acceptable limits

---

### Precision Limitations
- insufficient precision for required accuracy
- resource limitations affecting fidelity

---

## System Behavior

The system may:

- halt execution
- request clarification
- provide warnings
- suggest refinements

---

## Outcome

Failure Modes ensure that:

- invalid results are not silently produced
- users are informed of limitations
- system integrity is maintained
