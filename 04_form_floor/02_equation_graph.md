# Equation Graph

## Overview

The Equation Graph represents the relationships between mathematical expressions and variables.

It defines the structure of the problem in a way that supports evaluation and execution.

---

## Purpose

To:

- organize equations into a coherent structure
- define dependencies between variables
- determine evaluation order

---

## Structure

The Equation Graph consists of:

- nodes representing variables or expressions
- edges representing dependencies or relationships

---

## Characteristics

- directed
- acyclic (where applicable)
- structured for computation

---

## Role in the System

The Equation Graph enables:

- dependency resolution
- method selection
- efficient execution planning

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- constructs the graph from structured input
- validates connectivity and completeness
- identifies cycles or inconsistencies
- prepares the graph for execution

---

## Importance

Without a structured representation:

- relationships may be ambiguous
- execution may be inefficient or incorrect
- validation becomes difficult

---

## Outcome

The Equation Graph provides a **formal structure** that supports accurate and efficient computation.
