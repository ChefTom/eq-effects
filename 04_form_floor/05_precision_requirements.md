# Precision Requirements

## Overview

Precision Requirements define the level of numerical accuracy required for computation.

They ensure that results meet the desired level of fidelity.

---

## Purpose

To control:

- numerical accuracy
- stability of computation
- resource allocation

---

## Types of Precision

- global precision settings
- localized precision regions
- adaptive precision based on problem characteristics

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- interprets user-defined precision expectations
- applies domain-appropriate precision strategies
- adjusts computation accordingly

---

## Precision Governance

Precision may vary across the problem:

- higher precision in sensitive regions
- standard precision elsewhere

This allows efficient use of resources while maintaining accuracy.

---

## Role in Execution

Precision requirements influence:

- method selection
- solver configuration
- compute resource allocation

---

## Importance

Inadequate precision can lead to:

- inaccurate results
- instability
- loss of trust in outcomes

---

## Outcome

Precision Requirements ensure that computation is performed at the **appropriate level of accuracy**, aligned with user intent and problem characteristics.
