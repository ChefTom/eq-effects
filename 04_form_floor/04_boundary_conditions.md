# Boundary Conditions

## Overview

Boundary Conditions define the limits and constraints applied to the Mathematical Object.

They specify how the system behaves at the edges of the problem space.

---

## Purpose

To ensure that:

- the problem is well-defined
- solutions are physically or logically meaningful
- computation is constrained appropriately

---

## Types of Boundary Conditions

- initial conditions
- fixed-value boundaries
- dynamic or variable boundaries
- domain-specific constraints

---

## Sources

Boundary conditions may be:

- explicitly defined by the user
- inferred from context
- required by the mathematical model

---

## System Behavior

The system:

- integrates boundary conditions into the Mathematical Object
- validates consistency with equations and variables
- ensures completeness for execution

---

## Role in the System

Boundary Conditions influence:

- solution behavior
- method selection
- numerical stability

---

## Importance

Without proper boundary conditions:

- solutions may be undefined
- results may be incorrect
- interpretation becomes unreliable

---

## Outcome

Boundary Conditions ensure that the problem is **fully constrained and suitable for execution**.
