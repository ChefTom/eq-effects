# Glossary

## EQ Effects
A conceptual architecture for governed mathematical execution that transforms mathematical intent into computable, auditable, and reproducible form.

## Multipath Conversation Builder
The intake model that accepts mathematical work through near-natural written conversation, structured formula input, and context capture.

## Contextual Box
A structured context container that tracks assumptions, variables, units, constraints, intent, and execution goals.

## Contextual Processor
A governance component that normalizes and reconciles captured context into a consistent, execution-ready description.

## Mathematical Object
A formal, machine-usable representation of the user’s mathematical intent, constraints, and expected outputs.

## Equation Graph
A structured relationship map of equations, dependencies, and transformations within a Mathematical Object.

## Variable Registry
A canonical inventory of symbols, definitions, units, domains, and roles used in a Mathematical Object.

## Rich Result Package
A structured output package containing result values, method context, assumptions, warnings, and replay metadata.

## Computer as Solver
The principle that numerical execution is performed by compute systems; EQ Effects governs preparation, constraints, and transparency.

## Governance Layer
Cross-cutting controls for identity, auditability, precision policy, and reproducibility across the architecture.

## Replayability
The ability to rerun a mathematical workflow from preserved intent, context, method choices, and execution conditions.

## Precision Governance
Policies and controls that define precision requirements, numerical tolerance expectations, and acceptable uncertainty.

## LIME-compatible orchestration
Contextual orchestration compatible with LIME-style interaction patterns for structured intent capture and controlled flow.
